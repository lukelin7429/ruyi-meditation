# The Living Vessel: Why the Heart of Buddhism Beats in Traditional Characters

In his essay, Luke Lin reflects on the profound connection between Traditional Chinese characters and Buddhist wisdom. He emphasizes that common Chinese expressions carry centuries of Buddhist teachings within them. For example, phrases like "A blow and a shout" represent Zen master techniques, while "Seeing into one's true nature" captures the essence of Zen practice.

Lin argues that those raised with Chinese possess a natural advantage in accessing Buddhist teachings, as the religion traveled to China 2,000 years ago and was carefully preserved there. The Chinese Buddhist Canon remains "one of the most complete collections of both Mahayana and Theravada teachings in existence."

The author highlights Taiwan's decision to preserve Traditional Chinese characters while mainland China simplified them in the 1950s. This preservation allows modern readers to engage directly with ancient sutras "translated over a millennium ago" without requiring modern interpretation.

Lin expresses gratitude for maintaining this linguistic bridge to Buddhist wisdom. He describes feeling blessed with bilingual fluency and frames his mission as sharing these "ancient tools" with a global English-speaking audience, hoping to help people find "awakening and a life of genuine peace."
