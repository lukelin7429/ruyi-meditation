# Choosing Your Destination: Why not All Meditation is the Same

When people tell me they have started "meditating," my first question is often: "What is your goal?"

In my late twenties, I was first drawn to the Taoist tradition. My focus then was primarily on physical health and the allure of the "extraordinary." I practiced Internal Alchemy, learning to move Qi (vital energy) through specific parts of my body. While I made progress in energy control, the supernatural experiences I secretly hoped for never arrived. Later, I explored Yoga, which sought a "True Self" liberated from the material world.

Ultimately, I chose Buddhism. Why? Because it felt the most logical. Instead of searching for an elusive, unchanging "True Self"—which is difficult to prove or find—Buddhism views us as a dynamic flow of the five aggregates. It treats happiness like a science: if you want a specific result, you must cultivate the right causes. By using meditation to still the mind and wisdom to see reality as it is, we can systematically extinguish the roots of suffering.

To help you choose the path that aligns with your intent, here is a breakdown of how these three great traditions map the inner world.

## 1. THE TAOIST PATH: THE ALCHEMIST OF LIFE

Taoism views the body as a precious "vessel" or furnace. The goal is harmony and longevity.

- **The Method:** Refining Jing (essence) into Qi (energy), and Qi into Shen (spirit). This is known as "Internal Alchemy."
- **The Focus:** Dual cultivation of nature and life—balancing spiritual character with physical vitality to live in rhythm with the Dao (the Way).
- **The Goal:** To become a vessel of health, harmony, and, ultimately, spiritual immortality.

## 2. THE YOGA PATH: FINDING THE INNER DIAMOND

Yoga (in its classical Hindu sense) is a journey of discovery. It assumes that deep inside, you are already perfect and eternal.

- **The Philosophy:** It sees a divide between the Soul (Atman or Purusha) and Matter (Prakrti). The practice is about stripping away the "dirt" of the material world.
- **The Method:** Silencing the mind to reveal the eternal, unchanging, and divine "Self."
- **The Goal:** Union (Samadhi) with the Universal Consciousness. It is an achievement of "Fullness."

## 3. THE BUDDHIST PATH: THE ART OF DETACHMENT

Buddhism is a journey of deconstruction and awakening. It argues that the "Self" we cling to is a mental construct.

- **The Philosophy:** anatta and Dependent Origination. Nothing exists independently; everything is a flow of causes and conditions.
- **The Method:** Cultivating Samatha (calm) and Vipassanā (insight) to see the "Emptiness" of all things.
- **The Goal:** nibbāna—the extinction of the fires of greed, hatred, and delusion. It is about the cessation of the cycle of suffering.

## CONCLUSION: THE LOGIC OF YOUR GOAL

I have experienced the pleasures of energy work and the search for a "True Self," but I eventually chose Buddhism because I wanted a path of detachment and awakening rather than "enhancement." To me, the logic of the Five Aggregates provided a clearer map for navigating the reality of life.

Before you sit down to meditate tomorrow, ask yourself: What is my actual goal? Are you trying to refine your energy, discover a hidden soul, or extinguish the root causes of your suffering? Each of these directions yields vastly different results. Don't be a victim of blind faith or confusing terminology—choose the path that aligns with your intended destination and makes the most sense to your own heart and logic.
