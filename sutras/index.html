Mahāyāna Scriptures

Sūtras and treatises of the Great Vehicle大乘經典

The Mahāyāna — the "Great Vehicle" — is the stream of Buddhism that spread north and east into China, Korea, Japan, Tibet, and Vietnam. Its scriptures turn on two ideas: the bodhisattva, who vows to awaken for the sake of all beings, and emptiness (śūnyatā), the seeing that sets that vow free. These are the texts recited in the great monasteries of East Asia, the source of Chan and Zen, Pure Land and Huayan.

Each scripture here is presented as a plain-English doorway: what it teaches, why it matters, and how to begin reading it. Some entries lead directly to a full reading guide; others are prepared as study paths so the collection can grow one text, one chapter, and one life-question at a time.
