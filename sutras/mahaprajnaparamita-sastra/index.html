The Great Treatise on Wisdom.

The Mahāprajñāpāramitā Śāstra is one of the great monuments of Mahāyāna Buddhism: a vast explanation of the Perfection of Wisdom, traditionally attributed to Nāgārjuna and preserved in Kumārajīva's Chinese translation. This page is the entrance for reading it fascicle by fascicle.

Browse 100 fascicles → Study by theme →

How to read this collection

Each fascicle is treated as a study unit. The goal is not only to preserve the text, but to help an English reader see the argument, the practice point, and the relevance to ordinary life.

The individual fascicle essays will be filled in progressively. The structure is ready now, so readers can move through the treatise in order while the study materials grow.

What each essay will include

  • A plain-English overview of the fascicle.
  • Key terms from the Chinese text.
  • Major examples, arguments, and practice points.
  • Questions for reflection or class discussion.
