Why the Buddha Teaches Great Wisdom

This page begins Ru-Yi's own English translation of the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Śāstra. We translate slowly, section by section, with the Chinese source beside the English so readers can study the meaning, not merely read around it.

Translator: Ru-Yi Meditation Center Status: translation in progress Source base: Taishō T25, no. 1509 Begins with the treatise proper

Translation Method

Our aim is not to produce a decorative English paraphrase. We will keep the structure of Kumārajīva's Chinese, preserve important Buddhist terms, and then add plain-English explanations where a modern reader needs a handrail.

This English version begins with the treatise proper. We do not translate Sengrui's Chang'an preface here, since that preface belongs more to Chinese Buddhist literary and translation history than to a first reading of the treatise for modern English readers.

  • Key terms are translated consistently, with the Chinese term visible when useful.
  • Long arguments are divided into readable paragraphs without changing the reasoning.
  • Every completed section can be revised after review by Dharma teachers and readers familiar with the Chinese text.

Opening Homage

The treatise proper begins with homage to the Three Jewels: the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha. It then honors Maitreya, Śāriputra, and Subhūti before announcing the purpose of the work.

Chinese Source

智度大道佛從來，智度大海佛窮盡，智度相義佛無礙，稽首智度無等佛。

Ru-Yi Translation

The Buddha comes forth from the great path of the Perfection of Wisdom. The Buddha has fully sounded the great ocean of this wisdom. In the characteristics and meaning of this wisdom, the Buddha is unobstructed. I bow to the unequaled Buddha of the Perfection of Wisdom.

有無二見滅無餘，諸法實相佛所說，常住不壞淨煩惱，稽首佛所尊重法。

The two views of existence and nonexistence are completely ended. The Buddha teaches the true character of all dharmas: abiding, unbroken, and able to purify afflictions. I bow to the Dharma honored by the Buddha.

聖眾大海行福田，學無學人以莊嚴，後有愛種永已盡，我所既滅根亦除；已捨世間諸事業，種種功德所住處，一切眾中最為上，稽首真淨大德僧。

The noble Sangha is like a great ocean, practicing as a field of merit. It is adorned by those still in training and those beyond training. The seed of craving for future existence has been forever exhausted; the sense of “mine” has ceased, and its root has been removed.

They have left behind the affairs of the world and become a dwelling place for many virtues. Foremost among all assemblies, this Sangha is true, pure, and greatly virtuous. I bow to it.

一心恭敬三寶已，及諸救世彌勒等；智慧第一舍利弗，無諍空行須菩提。我今如力欲演說，大智彼岸實相義，願諸大德聖智人，一心善順聽我說！

Having single-mindedly honored the Three Jewels, and also Maitreya and the other saviors of the world, Śāriputra, foremost in wisdom, and Subhūti, who practices emptiness without conflict, I now wish, according to my ability, to explain the true meaning of the far shore of Great Wisdom. May all noble and wise ones listen with one mind, receptive and well-disposed.

Reading note: The opening presents wisdom as a crossing-over, not as cleverness. Prajñā cuts through fixed views, especially the tendency to grasp either “it truly exists” or “it does not exist at all.”

Question: Why Did the Buddha Teach the Great Perfection of Wisdom?

The question asks why the Buddha taught the Great Perfection of Wisdom Sutra. The treatise answers that Buddhas do not speak casually: a teaching of this scale must have a great cause.

Chinese Source

問曰：佛以何因緣故說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》？

Ru-Yi Translation

Question: For what causes and conditions did the Buddha teach the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra?

諸佛法不以無事及小因緣而自發言，譬如須彌山王不以無事及小因緣而動。今有何等大因緣故，佛說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》？

The Dharma of all Buddhas is not spoken without purpose, nor is it spoken for some small cause or condition. It is like Mount Sumeru, king of mountains: it does not move without purpose or for some small cause. What great causes and conditions, then, led the Buddha to teach the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra?

First Reason: To Reveal the Bodhisattva Path

Chinese Source

答曰：佛於三藏中，廣引種種諸喻，為聲聞說法，不說菩薩道。

Ru-Yi Translation

Answer: In the Three Baskets, the Buddha used many kinds of examples and taught the Dharma for the śrāvakas, but he did not teach the bodhisattva path.

唯《中阿含．本末經》中，佛記彌勒菩薩「汝當來世，當得作佛，號字彌勒」，亦不說種種菩薩行。

Only in the Beginning and End Sutra of the Middle Āgama did the Buddha give a prediction to the bodhisattva Maitreya, saying, “In a future age, you will become a Buddha, and your name will be Maitreya.” Even there, however, he did not explain the many practices of the bodhisattvas.

佛今欲為彌勒等廣說諸菩薩行，是故說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。

Now the Buddha wishes to explain the bodhisattva practices broadly for Maitreya and the others. For this reason, he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Reading note: The first reason is simple and important: the Perfection of Wisdom is taught because the bodhisattva path needs a fuller explanation. This sets the tone for the whole treatise. It is not only philosophy; it is a map for people who vow to awaken for the sake of all beings.

Second Reason: To Deepen Recollection of the Buddha

Chinese Source

復次，有菩薩修念佛三昧，佛為彼等欲令於此三昧得增益故說《般若波羅蜜經》。

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, there are bodhisattvas who cultivate Buddha-recollection samādhi. In order to help them increase and deepen this samādhi, the Buddha teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

如《般若波羅蜜．初品》中說：「佛現神足，放金色光明，遍照十方恒河沙等世界，示現大身，清淨光明，種種妙色滿虛空中。佛在眾中，端正殊妙，無能及者。譬如須彌山王，處於大海。」

As the first chapter of the Prajñāpāramitā says, the Buddha displays spiritual power, releases golden light, and illuminates worlds in the ten directions as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. He manifests a great body of pure radiance, filled with many wondrous colors, filling empty space. In the midst of the assembly, the Buddha is upright, beautiful, and beyond compare, like Mount Sumeru, king of mountains, standing in the great ocean.

諸菩薩見佛神變，於念佛三昧倍復增益，以是事故說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。

When the bodhisattvas see this transformation of the Buddha, their Buddha-recollection samādhi increases even more. For this reason, the Buddha teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Third Reason: Because Profound Dharma Was Part of the Original Turning

Chinese Source

復次，菩薩初生時，放大光明，普遍十方，行至七步，四顧觀察，作師子吼而說偈言：「我生胎分盡，是最末後身；我已得解脫，當復度眾生。」

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, when the Bodhisattva was first born, he emitted great light that filled the ten directions. He walked seven steps, looked around in the four directions, and uttered a lion's roar in verse:

“My share in the womb is finished. This is my final body. I have already attained liberation, and I will also liberate beings.”

作是誓已，身漸長大，欲捨親屬，出家修無上道。中夜起觀，見諸伎直、后妃婇女，狀若臭屍。即命車匿，令被白馬，夜半踰城，行十二由旬，到跋伽婆仙人所住林中，以刀剃髮，以上妙寶衣，貿麁布僧伽梨。

After making this vow, he gradually grew up. Wanting to leave his relatives and go forth to cultivate the unsurpassed path, he rose in the middle of the night and saw the entertainers, queens, and palace women as if they were foul corpses. He ordered Chandaka to saddle his white horse. At midnight he crossed the city wall, traveled twelve yojanas, and reached the forest where the sage Bhārgava lived. There he cut off his hair with a knife and exchanged his fine jeweled garments for a coarse saṅghāṭī robe.

於泥連禪河側，六年苦行，日食一麻，或食一米等，而自念言：「是處非道！」爾時，菩薩捨苦行處，到菩提樹下，坐金剛處。魔王將十八億萬眾來壞菩薩，菩薩以智慧功德力故，降魔眾已，即得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提。

Beside the Nairañjanā River, he practiced austerities for six years, eating only one sesame seed or one grain of rice each day. Then he reflected, “This is not the path.” Giving up the place of austerity, the Bodhisattva went to the bodhi tree and sat on the vajra seat. Māra, king of demons, brought eighteen koṭis of followers to disturb him. By the power of wisdom and merit, the Bodhisattva subdued Māra's army and attained unsurpassed, complete, perfect awakening.

是時三千大千世界主梵天王，名式棄，及色界諸天等，釋提桓因及欲界諸天等，并四天王，皆詣佛所，勸請世尊初轉法輪；亦是菩薩念本所願，及大慈大悲故，受請說法。諸法甚深者，般若波羅蜜是。以是故佛說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。

At that time Brahmā, lord of the three-thousand-great-thousand world-system, named Śikhin, together with the gods of the form realm, Śakra Devānām Indra, the gods of the desire realm, and the Four Heavenly Kings, all came to the Buddha and requested the Blessed One to first turn the wheel of Dharma. Remembering his original vow, and moved by great loving-kindness and great compassion, the Bodhisattva accepted their request and taught the Dharma.

Among the dharmas that are extremely profound, the perfection of wisdom is foremost. For this reason, the Buddha teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Fourth to Fifth Reasons: To Remove Doubt and Guide Those Who Can Be Liberated

Chinese Source

復次，有人疑佛不得一切智。所以者何？諸法無量無數，云何一人能知一切法？佛住般若波羅蜜實相清淨如虛空，無量無數法中，自發誠言：「我是一切智人。」欲斷一切眾生疑，以是故說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, some people doubt that the Buddha has attained all-knowledge. Why? They think, “Dharmas are immeasurable and countless. How could one person know all dharmas?”

The Buddha abides in the true character of the perfection of wisdom, pure like empty space. Among immeasurable and countless dharmas, he sincerely declares, “I am a person of all-knowledge.” Wishing to cut off the doubts of all beings, he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

復次，有眾生應得度者，以佛大功德智慧無量，難知難解故，為惡師所惑，心沒邪法，不入正道。為是輩人，起大慈心，以大悲手授之，令入佛道；是故自現最妙功德，出大神力。

Furthermore, there are beings who are ready to be liberated, but because the Buddha's great merit and wisdom are immeasurable, hard to know, and hard to understand, they are misled by bad teachers. Their minds sink into wrong teachings and do not enter the right path.

For such people, the Buddha gives rise to great loving-kindness. With the hand of great compassion, he takes hold of them and leads them into the Buddha path. Therefore he manifests the most wondrous virtues and displays great spiritual power.

如《般若波羅蜜．初品》中說：「佛入三昧王三昧，從三昧起，以天眼觀十方世界，舉身毛孔皆笑，從其足下千輻輪相，放六百千萬億種種色光明，從足指上至肉髻，處處各放六百千萬億種種色光明，普照十方無量無數如恒沙等諸佛世界，皆令大明。」佛從三昧起，欲宣示一切諸法實相，斷一切眾生疑結故，說《般若波羅蜜經》。

As the first chapter of the Prajñāpāramitā says, the Buddha enters the samādhi called King of Samādhis. Emerging from that samādhi, he observes the worlds of the ten directions with his divine eye. From all the pores of his body he smiles. From the thousand-spoked wheel marks on the soles of his feet, he releases six hundred thousand ten-thousands of koṭis of rays of many colors. From his toes up to the crown of his head, every place releases rays of many colors, illuminating immeasurable and countless buddha-worlds in the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges.

Emerging from samādhi, the Buddha wishes to reveal the true character of all dharmas and cut through the knots of doubt in all beings. For this reason, he teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Sixth to Ninth Reasons: To Inspire Faith, Trust, Joy, and Healing

Chinese Source

復次，有惡邪人，懷嫉妬意，誹謗言：「佛智慧不出於人，但以幻術惑世。」斷彼貢高邪慢意故，現無量神力、無量智慧力，於《般若波羅蜜》中，自說：「我神德無量，三界特尊，為一切覆護。若一發惡念，獲罪無量；一發淨信，受人天樂，必得涅槃果。」

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, there are crooked and harmful people who, out of jealousy, slander the Buddha, saying, “The Buddha's wisdom does not surpass ordinary human wisdom. He merely uses magical arts to deceive the world.”

To cut off their arrogance and wrong pride, the Buddha displays immeasurable spiritual power and immeasurable wisdom. In the Prajñāpāramitā he says, “My spiritual virtue is immeasurable. I am specially honored in the three realms and protect all beings. If someone gives rise to even one evil thought toward this, that person gains immeasurable fault. If someone gives rise to even one pure faith, that person receives the happiness of humans and devas and will certainly attain the fruit of nirvāṇa.”

復次，欲令人信受法故，言：「我是大師，有十力、四無所畏，安立聖主住處，心得自在，能師子吼，轉妙法輪，於一切世界最尊最上。」

Furthermore, in order to lead people to trust and accept the Dharma, the Buddha says, “I am the Great Teacher. I possess the ten powers and the four fearlessnesses. I abide in the dwelling place of the noble ones. My mind is free and sovereign. I utter the lion's roar and turn the wondrous wheel of Dharma. In all worlds, I am most honored and supreme.”

復次，佛世尊欲令眾生歡喜故，說是《般若波羅蜜經》言：「汝等應生大喜！何以故？一切眾生入邪見網，為異學惡師所惑，我於一切惡師邪網中得出。十力大師，難可值見，汝今已遇，我隨時開發三十七品等諸深法藏，恣汝採取。」

Furthermore, the Blessed Buddha teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra in order to bring beings joy. He says, “You should give rise to great joy. Why? All beings have entered the net of wrong views and are confused by bad teachers of other paths. I have escaped from all the wrong nets of bad teachers. The Great Teacher who possesses the ten powers is hard to encounter. Now you have met him. At the proper time, I open up the treasury of profound dharmas, such as the thirty-seven factors of awakening, so that you may gather from it as you wish.”

復次，一切眾生為結使病所煩惱，無始生死已來，無人能治此病者，常為外道惡師所誤。我今出世為大醫王，集諸法藥，汝等當服。是故佛說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。

Furthermore, all beings are troubled by the sickness of the fetters. Since beginningless birth and death, no one has been able to cure this sickness, and they have constantly been misled by bad teachers of other paths. “Now I appear in the world as the great king of physicians. I have gathered the medicines of the Dharma. You should take them.” For this reason, the Buddha teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Tenth Reason: To Show the Buddha's Inconceivable Body and Skillful Means

Chinese Source

復次，有人念言：「佛與人同，亦有生死，實受飢渴、寒熱、老病苦。」佛欲斷彼意故，說是《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。示言：「我身不可思議，梵天王等諸天祖父，於恒河沙等劫中，欲思量我身、尋究我聲，不能測度，況我智慧三昧？」如偈說：「諸法實相中，諸梵天王等，一切天地主，迷惑不能了！此法甚深妙，無能測量者，佛出悉開解，其明如日照。」

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, some people think, “The Buddha is the same as ordinary people. He too has birth and death and truly experiences the sufferings of hunger, thirst, cold, heat, aging, and sickness.” The Buddha wishes to cut off this thought, and so he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra, showing them: “My body is inconceivable.”

Even if Brahmā kings and the ancestor-gods of the devas tried, for kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, to measure my body and investigate my voice, they could not fathom them. How much less could they fathom my wisdom and samādhi?

As the verse says: “In the true character of all dharmas, the Brahmā kings and all rulers of heaven and earth are confused and cannot understand. This Dharma is extremely deep and wondrous. No one can measure it. When the Buddha appears, he opens and explains it all, and its brightness shines like the sun.”

又如佛初轉法輪時，應時菩薩從他方來，欲量佛身，上過虛空無量佛剎，至華上佛世界，見佛身如故，菩薩說言：「虛空無有邊，佛功德亦爾；設欲量其身，唐勞不能盡！上過虛空界，無量諸佛土，見釋師子身，如故而不異！佛身如金山，演出大光明，相好自莊嚴，猶如春華敷。」如佛身無量，光明、音響亦復無量，戒、定、慧等諸佛功德皆悉無量。如《密迹經》中三密，此中應廣說。

When the Buddha first turned the wheel of Dharma, a bodhisattva named Ying-shi came from another direction to measure the Buddha's body. He went upward through empty space, passing immeasurable buddha-lands, until he reached the world of Flower-Superior Buddha. There he saw that the Buddha's body was still the same. The bodhisattva said in verse, “Empty space has no boundary, and the Buddha's virtues are the same. If one tries to measure his body, one labors in vain and can never reach the end.”

Just as the Buddha's body is immeasurable, his light and voice are also immeasurable. The virtues of the Buddhas, such as discipline, samādhi, and wisdom, are all immeasurable.

復次，佛初生時，墮地行七步，口自發言，言竟便默，如諸嬰孩，不行不語，乳餔三歲，諸母養育，漸次長大。然佛身無數，過諸世間，為眾生故，現如凡人。凡人生時，身分諸根及其意識未成就故，身四威儀：坐、臥、行、住，言談語默，種種人法，皆悉未了；日月歲過，漸漸習學，能具人法。今佛云何生便能語能行，後更不能？以此致怪！

Furthermore, when the Buddha was first born, he fell to the ground, walked seven steps, and spoke by himself. After speaking, he became silent. Like other infants, he did not walk or speak. He was nursed and fed for three years by his mothers and was gradually raised. Yet the Buddha's body is countless and surpasses all worlds. For the sake of beings, he manifests as an ordinary human.

Ordinary people, when born, have not yet developed the body, the faculties, or consciousness. They do not yet understand the four postures of sitting, lying down, walking, and standing, nor speech, silence, and the many human ways. As days, months, and years pass, they gradually learn and become complete in human conduct. How then could the Buddha speak and walk at birth, but afterward no longer be able to do so? People might find this strange.

但為此故，以方便力，現行人法，如人威儀，令諸眾生信於深法。若菩薩生時，便能行能語，世人當作是念：「今見此人，世未曾有，必是天、龍、鬼、神，其所學法，必非我等所及。何以故？我等生死肉身，為結使業所牽，不得自在。如此深法，誰能及之？」以此自絕，不得成賢聖法器。

For this reason alone, by the power of skillful means, the Buddha manifests human conduct and ordinary human dignity, so that beings may have faith in the profound Dharma. If the Bodhisattva, at birth, could immediately walk and speak, people in the world would think, “This person is unlike anyone ever seen in the world. He must be a deva, nāga, spirit, or god. The Dharma he studies must be beyond our reach. We are fleshly bodies in birth and death, pulled along by fetters and karma, without freedom. Who could reach such profound Dharma?” In this way they would cut themselves off and would not become vessels for the Dharma of sages.

為是人故，於嵐毘尼園中生。雖即能至菩提樹下成佛，以方便力故，而現作孩童、幼小、年少、成人；於諸時中次第而受嬉戲、術藝、服御、五欲，具足人法。後漸見老、病、死苦，生厭患心，於夜中半，踰城出家，到鬱特伽阿羅洛仙人所，現作弟子而不行其法。

For the sake of such people, he was born in Lumbinī Garden. Although he could have gone directly to the bodhi tree and become a Buddha, by the power of skillful means he manifested as a child, a youth, and an adult. In each stage he experienced play, arts, skills, clothing, conveyances, the five desires, and the full range of human life. Later, gradually seeing the sufferings of aging, sickness, and death, he gave rise to disenchantment. At midnight he crossed the city wall and went forth. He went to the sage Udraka Rāmaputra and appeared as a disciple, though he did not practice that teacher's Dharma.

雖常用神通，自念宿命，迦葉佛時持戒行道，而今現修苦行六年求道。菩薩雖主三千大千世界，而現破魔軍，成無上道。隨順世法故，現是眾變。今於《般若波羅蜜》中現大神通智慧力故，諸人當知佛身無數，過諸世間。

Although he constantly used spiritual powers and remembered past lives, including how he upheld discipline and practiced the path in the time of Kāśyapa Buddha, he now manifested six years of austerity in search of the path. Although the Bodhisattva is lord of the three-thousand-great-thousand world-system, he manifested the defeat of Māra's army and the attainment of the unsurpassed path. In accord with worldly Dharma, he displayed all these transformations.

Now, in the Prajñāpāramitā, he manifests great spiritual power and the power of wisdom so that people will know: the Buddha's body is countless and surpasses all worlds.

Eleventh Reason: To Lead Beings from Extremes into the Middle Way

Chinese Source

復次，有人應可度者，或墮二邊；或以無智故，但求身樂；或有為道故，修著苦行。如是人等，於第一義中，失涅槃正道。佛欲拔此二邊，令入中道，故說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, among those who can be liberated, some fall into two extremes. Some, because of ignorance, seek only bodily pleasure. Others, taking that as the path, cling to the practice of painful austerities. Such people lose the right path of nirvāṇa within ultimate meaning. The Buddha wishes to pull them out of these two extremes and lead them into the middle way. Therefore he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Twelfth Reason: To Explain the Results of Honoring the Birth-Body and Dharma-Body

Chinese Source

復次，分別生身、法身供養果報故，說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》，如〈舍利塔品〉中說。

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra in order to explain the karmic results of making offerings to the birth-body and the Dharma-body, as described in the chapter on the reliquary stūpa.

Thirteenth Reason: To Explain Non-Retrogression and Its Marks

Chinese Source

復次，欲說阿鞞跋致，阿鞞跋致相故說。

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, he teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra because he wishes to explain non-retrogression and the marks of non-retrogression.

Fourteenth Reason: To Explain Māra's Illusions and Activities

Chinese Source

又為魔幻、魔事故說。

Ru-Yi Translation

Fifteenth Reason: To Support Future Offerings and Predictions of the Three Vehicles

Chinese Source

復次，為當來世人，供養般若波羅蜜因緣故；又欲授三乘記別故，說是《般若波羅蜜經》。如佛告阿難：「我涅槃後，此般若波羅蜜當至南方，從南方至西方，後五百歲中當至北方。是中多有信法善男子、善女人，種種華香、瓔珞、幢幡、伎樂、燈明、珍寶，以財物供養。若自書，若教人書，若讀誦、聽說，正憶念、修行，以法供養。是人以是因緣故，受種種世間樂；末後得三乘，入無餘涅槃。」如是等觀諸品中因緣事故，說《般若波羅蜜經》。

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, he teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra so that people in future generations may create causes and conditions by honoring the perfection of wisdom, and so that he may bestow predictions upon the three vehicles.

As the Buddha told Ānanda: “After my nirvāṇa, this perfection of wisdom will go to the south. From the south it will go to the west. In the latter five-hundred-year period, it will go to the north. There, many faithful good men and good women will honor it with many kinds of flowers, incense, necklaces, banners, music, lamps, jewels, and material gifts. Some will copy it themselves, teach others to copy it, read and recite it, listen to it being explained, remember it correctly, and practice it. In this way they make offerings through the Dharma. Because of these causes and conditions, they will receive many kinds of worldly happiness, and in the end they will attain one of the three vehicles and enter nirvāṇa without remainder.”

For such causes and conditions, as seen throughout the chapters, the Buddha teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Sixteenth Reason: To Teach the Four Siddhāntas

Chinese Source

復次，佛欲說第一義悉檀相故，說是《般若波羅蜜經》。有四種悉檀：一者、世界悉檀，二者、各各為人悉檀，三者、對治悉檀，四者、第一義悉檀。

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, the Buddha teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra because he wishes to explain the mark of the siddhānta of ultimate meaning. There are four kinds of siddhānta: first, the worldly siddhānta; second, the siddhānta taught for each individual person; third, the siddhānta of counteractive treatment; and fourth, the siddhānta of ultimate meaning.

四悉檀中總攝一切十二部經、八萬四千法藏，皆是實，無相違背。佛法中，有以世界悉檀故實，有以各各為人悉檀故實，有以對治悉檀故實，有以第一義悉檀故實。

Within these four siddhāntas, all the twelve divisions of scripture and the eighty-four thousand Dharma treasuries are true and do not contradict one another. In the Buddha Dharma, some things are true by worldly siddhānta; some are true by the siddhānta taught for individual persons; some are true by the siddhānta of counteractive treatment; and some are true by the siddhānta of ultimate meaning.

Worldly Siddhānta

Chinese Source

有世界者，有法從因緣和合故有，無別性。譬如車，轅、軸、輻、輞等和合故有，無別車。人亦如是，五眾和合故有，無別人。

Ru-Yi Translation

What is the worldly siddhānta? A thing exists because causes and conditions come together. It has no separate nature of its own. For example, a cart exists because the pole, axle, spokes, rim, and other parts come together. Apart from these, there is no separate cart. A person is the same: because the five aggregates come together, there is a person. Apart from the five aggregates, there is no separate person.

若無世界悉檀者，佛是實語人，云何言「我以清淨天眼，見諸眾生隨善惡業死此生彼受果報；善業者生天人中，惡業者墮三惡道」？復次，經言：「一人出世，多人蒙慶，福樂饒益，佛世尊也。」如《法句》中說：「神自能救神，他人安能救！神自行善智，是最能自救。」如《瓶沙王迎經》中佛說：「凡人不聞法，凡人著於我。」又《佛二夜經》中說：「佛初得道夜，至般涅槃夜，是二夜中間所說經教，一切皆實不顛倒。」若實無人者，佛云何言「我天眼見眾生」？是故當知有人者，世界悉檀，故非是第一義悉檀。

If there were no worldly siddhānta, then since the Buddha is a speaker of truth, how could he say, “With my pure divine eye, I see beings die here and be born there according to their good and bad karma, receiving their results. Those with good karma are born among devas and humans; those with bad karma fall into the three bad destinations”?

The sutras also say, “When one person appears in the world, many people receive celebration, blessing, happiness, and benefit. This is the Blessed Buddha.” The Dhammapada says, “One's own self can save oneself. How can another save one? When one practices good with wise understanding, this is the best way to save oneself.” Other sutras say, “Ordinary people who do not hear the Dharma cling to self,” and “From the night the Buddha first attained the path until the night of his parinirvāṇa, all the scriptures he taught between those two nights are true and not inverted.”

If there were truly no person at all, how could the Buddha say, “With my divine eye I see beings”? Therefore, when we say there is a person, this is by worldly siddhānta. It is not by the siddhānta of ultimate meaning.

問曰：第一悉檀是真實，實故名第一，餘者不應實。答曰：不然！是四悉檀各各有實，如如、法性、實際，世界悉檀故無，第一義悉檀故有。人等亦如是，世界悉檀故有，第一義悉檀故無。所以者何？人五眾因緣有故有是人等。譬如乳，色、香、味、觸因緣有故有是乳；若乳實無，乳因緣亦應無。今乳因緣實有故，乳亦應有；非如一人第二頭、第三手，無因緣而有假名。如是等相，名為世界悉檀。

Someone asks: “The first siddhānta, ultimate meaning, is what is truly real. Because it is real, it is called first. The others should not be called real.” The answer is: Not so. Each of the four siddhāntas has its own reality. Suchness, Dharma-nature, and the real limit are nonexistent by worldly siddhānta, but existent by ultimate siddhānta. Persons and other such things are the same: they exist by worldly siddhānta, but do not exist by ultimate siddhānta.

Why? Because a person exists through the causes and conditions of the five aggregates. It is like milk: because the causes and conditions of color, smell, taste, and touch exist, there is milk. If milk truly did not exist, the causes and conditions of milk should also not exist. But since the causes and conditions of milk truly exist, milk too should exist. It is not like a second head or third hand on one person, which is only a false name with no causes and conditions. Such marks are called worldly siddhānta.

Siddhānta Taught for Each Person

Chinese Source

云何各各為人悉檀者？觀人心行而為說法，於一事中，或聽或不聽。如經中所說：「雜報業故，雜生世間，得雜觸、雜受。」更有《破群那經》中說：「無人得觸，無人得受。」問曰：此二經云何通？答曰：以有人疑後世，不信罪福，作不善行，墮斷滅見；欲斷彼疑，捨彼惡行，欲拔彼斷見，是故說雜生世間、雜觸、雜受。是破群那計有我有神，墮計常中。破群那問佛言：「大德！誰受？」若佛說「某甲某甲受」，便墮計常中，其人我見倍復牢固，不可移轉，以是故不說有受者、觸者。如是等相，是名各各為人悉檀。

Ru-Yi Translation

What is the siddhānta taught for each individual person? The Buddha observes a person's mental tendencies and then teaches the Dharma accordingly. In regard to one matter, he may permit it for one person and not permit it for another.

For example, one sutra says, “Because of mixed-result karma, beings are born into the mixed world and receive mixed contacts and mixed feelings.” Yet the Phagguna Sutra says, “There is no person who obtains contact, and no person who obtains feeling.”

Question: How can these two sutras be reconciled? Answer: Some people doubt future lives, do not believe in fault and merit, commit unwholesome actions, and fall into the view of annihilation. To cut off their doubt, make them abandon bad conduct, and pull out their annihilationist view, the Buddha says that beings are born into the mixed world and receive mixed contacts and mixed feelings.

Phagguna, however, held the view that there is a self, a spirit, and he fell into eternalism. Phagguna asked the Buddha, “Great Virtuous One, who receives feeling?” If the Buddha had said, “So-and-so receives it,” Phagguna would have fallen further into eternalism, and his view of self and person would have become even more solid and immovable. Therefore the Buddha did not say that there is a receiver of feeling or a receiver of contact. Such marks are called the siddhānta taught for each individual person.

Counteractive Siddhānta

Chinese Source

對治悉檀者，有法，對治則有，實性則無。譬如重、熱、膩、酢、醎藥草飲食等，於風病中名為藥，於餘病非藥；若輕、冷、甘、苦、澁藥草飲食等，於熱病名為藥，於餘病非藥；若輕、辛、苦、澁、熱藥草飲食等，於冷病中名為藥，於餘病非藥。

Ru-Yi Translation

What is counteractive siddhānta? A Dharma may exist as a remedy, while by real nature it does not exist. For example, heavy, hot, oily, sour, and salty medicines, herbs, foods, and drinks are called medicine for wind illness, but not for other illnesses. Light, cool, sweet, bitter, and astringent medicines, herbs, foods, and drinks are called medicine for heat illness, but not for other illnesses. Light, pungent, bitter, astringent, and hot medicines, herbs, foods, and drinks are called medicine for cold illness, but not for other illnesses.

佛法中治心病亦如是：不淨觀思惟，於貪欲病中，名為善對治法；於瞋恚病中，不名為善，非對治法。所以者何？觀身過失，名不淨觀；若瞋恚人觀過失者，則增益瞋恚火故。思惟慈心，於瞋恚病中，名為善對治法；於貪欲病中，不名為善，非對治法。所以者何？慈心於眾生中求好事、觀功德；若貪欲人求好事、觀功德者，則增益貪欲故。

The treatment of mental illness in the Buddha Dharma is the same. Reflection on impurity is a good counteractive Dharma for the illness of lust, but it is not good for the illness of anger. Why? Reflection on impurity observes the faults of the body. If an angry person observes faults, this increases the fire of anger.

Reflection on loving-kindness is a good counteractive Dharma for the illness of anger, but it is not good for the illness of lust. Why? Loving-kindness seeks what is good in beings and observes their virtues. If a lustful person seeks what is good and observes virtues, this increases lust.

因緣觀法，於愚癡病中，名為善對治法；於貪欲、瞋恚病中，不名為善，非對治法。所以者何？先邪觀故生邪見，邪見即是愚癡。問曰：如佛法中說十二因緣甚深，如說：「佛告阿難：『是因緣法甚深，難見難解，難覺難觀，細心巧慧人乃能解。』」愚癡人於淺近法，猶尚難解，何況甚深因緣？今云何言「愚癡人應觀因緣法」？答曰：愚癡人者，非謂如牛羊等愚癡；是人欲求實道，邪心觀故，生種種邪見。如是愚癡人，當觀因緣，是名為善對治法。

Contemplation of causes and conditions is a good counteractive Dharma for the illness of delusion, but it is not good for the illnesses of lust and anger. Why? Because wrong views first arise from wrong contemplation, and wrong view is delusion.

Someone asks: “The Buddha Dharma says the twelve links of dependent arising are extremely profound. The Buddha told Ānanda, ‘This Dharma of causes and conditions is extremely profound, hard to see, hard to understand, hard to awaken to, and hard to contemplate. Only people with subtle minds and skillful wisdom can understand it.’ Deluded people have difficulty understanding even shallow and nearby dharmas. How much more difficult is profound dependent arising? How can you now say that deluded people should contemplate causes and conditions?”

The answer is: Deluded people here does not mean deluded like cattle or sheep. It refers to people who seek the true path but, because they contemplate with a wrong mind, give rise to many wrong views. For such deluded people, contemplation of causes and conditions is a good counteractive Dharma.

若行瞋恚、婬欲人，欲求樂、欲惱他，於此人中，非善非對治法；不淨、慈心思惟，是二人中，是善是對治法。何以故？是二觀能拔瞋恚、貪欲毒刺故。

For people ruled by anger or lust, who seek pleasure or wish to trouble others, this is not a good counteractive Dharma. For those two kinds of people, reflection on impurity and loving-kindness are good counteractive Dharmas, because these two contemplations pull out the poisonous thorns of anger and lust.

復次，著常顛倒眾生，不知諸法相似相續有；如是人觀無常，是對治悉檀，非第一義。何以故？一切諸法自性空故。如說偈言：「無常見有常，是名為顛倒；空中無無常，何處見有常？」

Furthermore, beings who are attached to the inverted view of permanence do not understand that dharmas exist in a similar and continuous succession. For such people, contemplation of impermanence is counteractive siddhānta, but not ultimate meaning. Why? Because the nature of all dharmas is empty.

As the verse says: “Seeing permanence in what is impermanent is called inversion. In emptiness, there is no impermanence. Where then could permanence be seen?”

問曰：一切有為法，皆無常相應，是第一義，云何言無常非實？所以者何？一切有為法，生、住、滅相，前生、次住、後滅故，云何言無常非實？答曰：有為法不應有三相。何以故？三相不實故。若諸法生、住、滅是有為相者，今「生」中亦應有三相，「生」是有為相故；如是一一處亦應有三相，是則無窮，住、滅亦如是。若諸生、住、滅各更無有生、住、滅者，不應名有為法。何以故？有為法相無故。以是故，諸法無常，非第一義。

Someone asks: “All conditioned dharmas are connected with the mark of impermanence. This is ultimate meaning. Why do you say impermanence is not real? All conditioned dharmas have the marks of arising, abiding, and ceasing: first arising, next abiding, and later ceasing. How can you say impermanence is not real?”

The answer is: Conditioned dharmas should not have three marks. Why? Because the three marks are not real. If arising, abiding, and ceasing are the marks of conditioned dharmas, then arising itself should also have these three marks, since arising is a conditioned mark. Each and every place would likewise have three marks, and this would lead to infinite regress. The same is true of abiding and ceasing. If each arising, abiding, and ceasing does not itself have arising, abiding, and ceasing, then they should not be called conditioned dharmas. Why? Because the marks of conditioned dharmas would be absent.

復次，若一切實性無常，則無行業報。何以故？無常名生滅失故，譬如腐種子不生果。如是則無行業，無行業云何有果報？今一切賢聖法有果報，善智之人所可信受，不應言無。以是故，諸法非無常性。如是等無量因緣說，不得言諸法無常性。一切有為法無常，苦、無我等亦如是。如是等相，名為對治悉檀。

Therefore, the impermanence of dharmas is not ultimate meaning. Furthermore, if all real natures were impermanent, then there would be no karmic action and result. Why? Impermanence means loss through arising and ceasing. It is like a rotten seed that cannot produce fruit. If this were so, there would be no action. Without action, how could there be result? Yet all dharmas of sages have results, and wise people can trust and accept this. It should not be denied.

Therefore, dharmas do not have impermanence as their real nature. For countless reasons such as these, one cannot say that dharmas have impermanence as their nature. What is said about all conditioned dharmas being impermanent is also true of suffering, no-self, and so on. Such marks are called counteractive siddhānta.

Ultimate Siddhānta

Chinese Source

第一義悉檀者，一切法性，一切論議語言，一切是法非法，一一可分別破散；諸佛、辟支佛、阿羅漢所行真實法，不可破，不可散。上於三悉檀中所不通者，此中皆通。問曰：云何通？答曰：所謂通者，離一切過失，不可變易，不可勝。何以故？除第一義悉檀，諸餘論議，諸餘悉檀，皆可破故。

Ru-Yi Translation

What is the siddhānta of ultimate meaning? The nature of all dharmas, all debates and language, all claims that something is Dharma or not Dharma, can each be analyzed, broken apart, and dispersed. But the true Dharma practiced by the Buddhas, pratyekabuddhas, and arhats cannot be broken apart and cannot be dispersed. Whatever could not be reconciled in the previous three siddhāntas is reconciled here.

Question: What does “reconciled” mean? Answer: It means being free from every fault, unable to be changed, and unable to be overcome. Why? Apart from the siddhānta of ultimate meaning, all other debates and all other siddhāntas can be broken apart.

如《眾義經》中所說偈：「各各自依見，戲論起諍競；若能知彼非，是為知正見。不肯受他法，是名愚癡人，作是論議者，真是愚癡人。若依自是見，而生諸戲論，若此是淨智，無非淨智者。」此三偈中，佛說第一義悉檀相。

As the Sutra of Many Meanings says in verse: “Each relying on one's own view, conceptual elaboration gives rise to dispute. If one can know that this is not so, this is to know right view. Refusing to accept another's Dharma is called being deluded. One who argues in this way is truly a deluded person. If one relies on one's own view as right and gives rise to conceptual elaborations, if this were pure wisdom, then there would be nothing that is not pure wisdom.”

所謂世間眾生自依見、自依法、自依論議而生諍競；戲論即諍競本，戲論依諸見生。如說偈言：「有受法故有諸論，若無有受何所論？有受無受諸見等，是人於此悉已除。」行者能如實知此者，於一切法、一切戲論，不受不著，不見是實，不共諍競，能知佛法甘露味。若不爾者，則謗法。

In these three verses, the Buddha teaches the mark of the siddhānta of ultimate meaning. Beings in the world rely on their own views, their own dharmas, and their own arguments, and from these arise disputes. Conceptual elaboration is the root of dispute, and conceptual elaboration arises depending on views.

As another verse says: “Because there is grasping of dharmas, there are arguments. If there were no grasping, what would there be to argue about? The views of grasping and non-grasping, and all such views, have been removed in this person.”

If practitioners can know this as it really is, then with regard to all dharmas and all conceptual elaborations, they do not receive them, do not cling to them, do not see them as real, and do not enter into dispute. They are able to know the sweet nectar taste of the Buddha Dharma. If they do not know this, they slander the Dharma.

若不受他法，不知不取，是無智人。若爾者，應一切論議人皆無智。何以故？各各不相受法故。所謂有人自謂法第一義淨，餘人妄語不淨。譬如世間治法，故治法者，刑罰殺戮，種種不淨，世間人信受行之，以為真淨；於餘出家善聖人中，是最為不淨。

If someone refuses another's Dharma, does not know it, and does not accept it, that person lacks wisdom. If so, then all people engaged in debate would lack wisdom. Why? Because each refuses to accept the Dharma of the others. Some people claim that their own Dharma is the pure ultimate meaning, while others speak falsely and impurely. But in worldly laws, those who administer punishment, execution, and many impure acts are trusted and followed by people of the world as genuinely pure. Among renunciants and good noble people, however, such things are most impure.

外道出家人法，五熱中一腳立、拔髮等，尼犍子輩以為妙慧，餘人說此為癡法。如是等種種外道出家、白衣婆羅門法，各各自以為好，餘皆妄語。

Among non-Buddhist renunciants, some stand on one foot amid five fires, pull out their hair, and practice other austerities. The Nirgrantha followers consider these to be wondrous wisdom, while others call them deluded practices. In this way, many kinds of non-Buddhist renunciants, householders, and brahmins each consider their own practices good and all others false.

是佛法中亦有犢子比丘說：「如四大和合有眼法，如是五眾和合有人法。」《犢子阿毘曇》中說：「五眾不離人，人不離五眾，不可說五眾是人、離五眾是人，人是第五不可說法藏中所攝。」說一切有道人輩言：「神人，一切種、一切時、一切法門中求不可得，譬如兔角龜毛常無。復次十八界、十二入、五眾實有，而此中無人。」更有佛法中方廣道人言：「一切法不生不滅，空無所有，譬如兔角龜毛常無。」

Even within the Buddha Dharma there are Vātsīputrīya monks who say, “Just as the eye-dharma exists from the combination of the four great elements, so too the person-dharma exists from the combination of the five aggregates.” In the Vātsīputrīya Abhidharma it is said, “The five aggregates are not separate from the person, and the person is not separate from the five aggregates. It cannot be said that the five aggregates are the person, nor that the person is apart from the five aggregates. The person is included in the fifth, inexpressible Dharma treasury.”

Those who follow the path that says all exists say, “A spirit-person cannot be found in any mode, at any time, or in any Dharma-door. It is like rabbit horns or turtle hair, always nonexistent. Moreover, the eighteen elements, twelve sense bases, and five aggregates truly exist, but there is no person within them.” Others within the Buddha Dharma, followers of the Vaipulya path, say, “All dharmas are unborn and unceasing, empty, and without anything at all, like rabbit horns or turtle hair, always nonexistent.”

如是等一切論議師輩，自守其法，不受餘法，此是實，餘者妄語。若自受其法，自法供養，自法修行，他法不受、不供養，為作過失。若以是為清淨得第一義利者，則一切無非清淨。何以故？彼一切皆自愛法故。

All such debaters guard their own Dharma and do not accept other Dharmas. They say, “This is real; the rest is false.” If they accept only their own Dharma, honor only their own Dharma, practice only their own Dharma, and neither accept nor honor others, this becomes a fault. If this were purity and the benefit of ultimate meaning, then nothing would fail to be pure. Why? Because all of them love their own Dharma.

問曰：若諸見皆有過失，第一義悉檀何者是？答曰：過一切語言道，心行處滅，遍無所依，不示諸法。諸法實相，無初、無中、無後，不盡、不壞，是名第一義悉檀。如摩訶衍義偈中說：「語言盡竟，心行亦訖；不生不滅，法如涅槃。說諸行處，名世界法；說不行處，名第一義。一切實一切非實，及一切實亦非實，一切非實非不實，是名諸法之實相。」如是等處處經中說第一義悉檀。是義甚深，難見難解，佛欲說是義故，說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。

Question: If all views have faults, which one is the siddhānta of ultimate meaning? Answer: It is beyond every path of language. The range of mental activity has ceased. It depends on nothing anywhere and does not display any dharma. The true character of all dharmas has no beginning, no middle, and no end. It is not exhausted and not destroyed. This is called the siddhānta of ultimate meaning.

As the verses on the meaning of the Mahāyāna say: “Language has come to an end; mental activity also has ceased. Unborn and unceasing, dharmas are like nirvāṇa. To speak of places where action moves is called worldly Dharma; to speak of the place where action does not move is called ultimate meaning. All is real, all is not real; all is both real and not real; all is neither real nor not real. This is called the true character of all dharmas.”

In passages such as these, the scriptures teach the siddhānta of ultimate meaning. This meaning is extremely deep, hard to see, and hard to understand. Because the Buddha wishes to teach this meaning, he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Seventeenth Reason: To Bring Great Debaters to Faith in the Buddha Dharma

Chinese Source

復次，欲令長爪梵志等大論議師於佛法中生信故，說是《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。有梵志號名長爪，更有名先尼婆蹉衢多羅，更有名薩遮迦摩揵提等。是等閻浮提大論議師輩言：「一切論可破，一切語可壞，一切執可轉故，無有實法可信可恭敬者。」

Furthermore, the Buddha teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra in order to bring great debaters such as the brahmin Long Nails to faith in the Buddha Dharma. There was a brahmin named Long Nails; there were also others named Śreṇika Vatsagotra, Satyaka Nirgranthīputra, and so on. These great debaters of Jambudvīpa said, “Every doctrine can be refuted, every statement can be broken down, and every attachment can be overturned. Long Nails thought to himself, “In the past I was arrogant and was defeated by my sister. Now these people also look down on me and insult me. Because of these two things, I make this vow: I will not cut my nails until I have finished reading the eighteen kinds of scriptures.” When people saw that his nails were long, they called him the brahmin Long Nails. With the power of wisdom gained from many scriptures, he criticized in every way what was Dharma and what was not Dharma, what was fitting and what was not fitting, what was real and what was not real, what existed and what did not exist, and he defeated the arguments of others. He was like a powerful mad elephant charging and trampling, with no one able to restrain him.

如是長爪梵志以論議力，摧伏諸論師已，還至摩伽陀國王舍城那羅聚落，至本生處，問人言：「我姊生子，今在何處？」有人語言：「汝姊子者，適生八歲，讀一切經書盡；至年十六，論議勝一切人；有釋種道人姓瞿曇，與作弟子。」長爪聞之，即起憍慢，生不信心，而作是言：「如我姊子聰明如是，彼以何術，誘誑剃頭作弟子？」

After Long Nails had used the power of debate to subdue many debaters, he returned to Magadha, to Rājagṛha, to the village of Nālaka, his birthplace. He asked people, “Where is the son born to my sister now?” Someone said, “Your sister's son, when he had just reached eight years old, had already finished reading all the scriptures. By the age of sixteen, he surpassed everyone in debate. A Śākya renunciant surnamed Gautama has made him his disciple.” When Long Nails heard this, arrogance arose at once and he gave rise to disbelief. He said, “Since my sister's son is so intelligent, by what technique did that man entice and deceive him into shaving his head and becoming a disciple?”

說是語已，直向佛所。爾時，舍利弗初受戒半月，佛邊侍立，以扇扇佛。長爪梵志見佛，問訊訖，一面坐，作是念：「一切論可破，一切語可壞，一切執可轉，是中何者是諸法實相？何者是第一義？何者性？何者相？不顛倒？」如是思惟，譬如大海水中，欲盡其涯底，求之既久，不得一法實可以入心者。

Having said this, he went straight to the Buddha. At that time Śāriputra had been newly ordained for half a month. He was standing beside the Buddha, fanning him with a fan. Long Nails saw the Buddha, greeted him, and sat to one side. He thought, “Every doctrine can be refuted, every statement can be broken down, and every attachment can be overturned. Among these, what is the true character of all dharmas? What is ultimate meaning? What is their nature? What is their mark? What is not inverted?” Thinking in this way, he was like someone in the waters of the great ocean who wanted to reach all the way to its shore and bottom. After searching for a long time, he could not find even one real dharma that his mind could enter into.

「彼以何論議道而得我姊子？」作是思惟已，而語佛言：「瞿曇！我一切法不受。」佛問長爪：「汝一切法不受，是見受不？」佛所質義，汝已飲邪見毒，今出是毒氣，言：「一切法不受，是見汝受不？」爾時，長爪梵志如好馬見鞭影即覺，便著正道；長爪梵志亦如是，得佛語鞭影入心，即棄捐貢高，慚愧低頭。

“By what path of debate did he win over my sister's son?” After thinking this, he said to the Buddha, “Gautama, I accept no dharma at all.” The Buddha asked Long Nails, “You accept no dharma at all. Do you accept this view?” The meaning of the Buddha's question was this: “You have already drunk the poison of wrong views, and now you exhale its poisonous fumes, saying, ‘I accept no dharma at all.’ Do you accept this view?” At that time the brahmin Long Nails was like a fine horse that awakens as soon as it sees the shadow of the whip and immediately takes to the right road. Long Nails was the same: when the shadow of the Buddha's words entered his mind, he immediately cast off his arrogance, felt shame, and lowered his head.

如是思惟：「佛置我著二處負門中：若我說是見我受，是負處門麁，故多人知，云何自言一切法不受，今受是見？此是現前妄語，是麁負處門，多人所知。第二負處門細，我欲受之，以不多人知故。」作是念已，答佛言：「瞿曇！一切法不受，是見亦不受。」佛語梵志：「汝不受一切法，是見亦不受，則無所受，與眾人無異，何用自高而生憍慢？」

He thought, “The Buddha has placed me inside two doors of defeat. If I say, ‘I accept this view,’ that door of defeat is coarse, and many people will know it. How could I say, ‘I accept no dharma at all,’ and now accept this view? This would be an obvious falsehood, a coarse door of defeat known by many. The second door of defeat is subtle. I will accept that one, because not many people will know it.” After thinking this, he answered the Buddha, “Gautama, I accept no dharma at all, and I also do not accept this view.” The Buddha said to the brahmin, “You do not accept any dharma, and you also do not accept this view. Then there is nothing you accept, and you are no different from everyone else. What use is there in exalting yourself and giving rise to arrogance?”

如是，長爪梵志不能得答，自知墮負處，即於佛一切智中起恭敬，生信心，自思惟：「我墮負處，世尊不彰我負，不言是非，不以為意；佛心柔濡，第一清淨；一切語論處滅，得大甚深法，是可恭敬處；心淨第一。」佛說法斷其邪見故，即於坐處得遠塵離垢，諸法中得法眼淨。時，舍利弗聞是語，得阿羅漢。是長爪梵志出家作沙門，得大力阿羅漢。

In this way, the brahmin Long Nails could not answer. Knowing himself that he had fallen into a defeated position, he gave rise to reverence for the Buddha's all-knowledge and produced faith. He thought, “I have fallen into defeat, yet the World-Honored One does not expose my defeat, does not say who is right or wrong, and does not take it to heart. The Buddha's mind is gentle and yielding, foremost in purity. The place where all statements and arguments cease is where he has attained the great, profound Dharma. This is worthy of reverence; his mind is foremost in purity.” Because the Buddha taught the Dharma and cut off his wrong views, while still seated there he became far from dust and free from defilement, and with respect to dharmas he attained the pure Dharma-eye. At that time Śāriputra heard these words and became an arhat. The brahmin Long Nails left home, became a renunciant, and attained the state of a powerful arhat.

若長爪梵志不聞般若波羅蜜氣分，離四句第一義相應法，小信尚不得，何況得出家道果？佛欲導引如是等大論議師利根人故，說是《般若波羅蜜經》。

If the brahmin Long Nails had not heard even a trace of the Prajñāpāramitā, the Dharma corresponding to ultimate meaning that is apart from the four propositions, he could not have gained even a small measure of faith. How much less could he have attained the fruit of the path after leaving home? Because the Buddha wished to guide great debaters and sharp-minded people such as these, he taught the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra.

Eighteenth to Twenty-First Reasons: To Reveal the Dharma Beyond Dispute and Fixed Categories

Chinese Source

復次，諸佛有二種說法：一者、觀人心隨可度者；二者、觀諸法相。

今佛欲說諸法實相故，說是《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。如說〈相不相品〉中，諸天子問佛：「是般若波羅蜜甚深，云何作相？」佛告諸天子：「空則是相，無相、無作相、無生滅相、無行之相，常不生、如性相、寂滅相等。」

Ru-Yi Translation

Furthermore, the Buddhas teach Dharma in two ways: first, by observing people's minds and following what can liberate them; second, by observing the marks of dharmas.

Now the Buddha wishes to teach the true character of all dharmas, and therefore he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra. As the chapter “Marks and Non-Marks” says, the devas' sons asked the Buddha, “This Prajñāpāramitā is extremely deep. What is its mark?” The Buddha told the devas' sons, “Emptiness itself is its mark: the mark of signlessness, the mark of wishlessness, the mark of non-arising and non-ceasing, the mark of non-activity, the mark of constant non-arising, the mark of suchness, the mark of quiescent cessation, and so on.”

復次，有二種說法：一者、諍處，二者、不諍處。

諍處，如餘經中說；今欲明無諍處故，說是《般若波羅蜜經》。有相、無相，有物、無物，有依、無依，有對、無對，有上、無上，世界、非世界，亦如是。

Furthermore, there are two kinds of Dharma teaching: first, a place of dispute; second, a place without dispute.

Places of dispute are taught in other sutras. Here, because the Buddha wishes to clarify the place without dispute, he teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra. Having marks and having no marks, having something and having nothing, depending on something and depending on nothing, being opposed and being unopposed, being surpassed and unsurpassed, being worldly and not worldly, and so forth, are all like this.

問曰：佛大慈悲心，但應說無諍法，何以說諍法？

答曰：無諍法皆是無相，常寂滅不可說；今說布施等，及無常、苦、空等諸法，皆為寂滅無戲論故說。利根者知佛意，不起諍；鈍根者不知佛意，取相、著心故起諍。

此般若波羅蜜，諸法畢竟空故，無諍處；若畢竟空可得可諍者，不名畢竟空。是故，《般若波羅蜜經》名無諍處，有無二事皆寂滅故。

Question: Since the Buddha has a mind of great loving-kindness and compassion, he should only teach the Dharma without dispute. Why does he teach the Dharma that becomes a place of dispute?

Answer: The Dharma without dispute is entirely without marks, constantly quiescent and impossible to speak. When the Buddha now teaches giving and other practices, and dharmas such as impermanence, suffering, and emptiness, he teaches all of them for the sake of quiescence and freedom from conceptual elaboration. Those of sharp faculties understand the Buddha's intention and do not give rise to dispute. Those of dull faculties do not understand the Buddha's intention; because they seize upon marks and attach their minds, dispute arises.

This Prajñāpāramitā is the place without dispute, because all dharmas are ultimately empty. If ultimate emptiness could be obtained and disputed over, it would not be called ultimate emptiness. Therefore the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra is called the place without dispute, because both existence and nonexistence are quiescently extinguished.

復次，餘經中多以三種門說諸法，所謂善門，不善門，無記門；今欲說非善門、非不善門、非無記門諸法相故，說《摩訶般若波羅蜜經》。學法、無學法、非學非無學法；見諦斷法、思惟斷法、無斷法；可見有對，不可見有對、不可見無對；上、中、下法；小、大、無量法；如是等三法門亦如是。

Furthermore, in other sutras the Buddha often teaches dharmas through three gateways: the gateway of what is wholesome, the gateway of what is unwholesome, and the gateway of what is indeterminate. Here, because he wishes to teach the marks of dharmas that are not the wholesome gateway, not the unwholesome gateway, and not the indeterminate gateway, he teaches the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra. The dharmas of learners, the dharmas of those beyond learning, and dharmas that are neither learning nor beyond learning; dharmas abandoned by seeing the truths, dharmas abandoned by cultivation, and dharmas not to be abandoned; visible and resistant, invisible and resistant, invisible and non-resistant; higher, middle, and lower dharmas; small, great, and immeasurable dharmas; and other threefold Dharma gateways are also like this.

復次，餘經中說四念處，隨聲聞法門。於是比丘觀內身三十六物，除欲貪病；如是觀外身，觀內外身。今於四念處，欲以異門說般若波羅蜜。如所說：菩薩觀內身，於身不生覺觀，不得身，以無所得故。如是觀外身，觀內外身，於身不生覺觀，不得身，以無所得故。

於身念處中觀身而不生身覺觀，是事甚難。

三念處亦如是。四正勤、四如意足、四禪、四諦等種種四法門，亦如是。

Furthermore, in other sutras the Buddha teaches the four establishments of mindfulness according to the Dharma gateway of the śrāvakas. There, a monk observes the thirty-six substances inside the body in order to remove the illness of desire and attachment. In the same way he observes the external body, and the internal and external body. Now, with regard to the four establishments of mindfulness, the Buddha wishes to teach Prajñāpāramitā through a different gateway. As it is said: when a bodhisattva observes the internal body, with regard to the body he does not give rise to conceptual observation of the body and does not obtain the body, because there is nothing to be obtained. In the same way, when he observes the external body and the internal and external body, with regard to the body he does not give rise to conceptual observation of the body and does not obtain the body, because there is nothing to be obtained.

Within the establishment of mindfulness of the body, to observe the body without giving rise to conceptual observation of the body is extremely difficult.

The other three establishments of mindfulness are the same. The four right efforts, the four bases of spiritual power, the four dhyānas, the four truths, and the various fourfold Dharma gateways are also like this.

復次，餘經中佛說五眾，無常、苦、空、無我相；今於是五眾欲說異法門故，說《般若波羅蜜經》。如佛告須菩提：「菩薩若觀色是常行，不行般若波羅蜜；受、想、行、識是常行，不行般若波羅蜜；色無常行，不行般若波羅蜜；受、想、行、識無常行，不行般若波羅蜜。」五受眾、五道，如是等種種五法門亦如是。餘六、七、八等，乃至無量法門亦如是。

如摩訶般若波羅蜜無量無邊，說《般若波羅蜜》因緣，亦無量無邊。是事廣故，今略說摩訶般若波羅蜜因緣起法竟。

Furthermore, in other sutras the Buddha teaches the five aggregates as marked by impermanence, suffering, emptiness, and no-self. Here, because he wishes to teach a different Dharma gateway with regard to these five aggregates, he teaches the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra. As the Buddha told Subhūti: “If a bodhisattva observes form as a practice of permanence, he is not practicing Prajñāpāramitā. If he observes feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness as a practice of permanence, he is not practicing Prajñāpāramitā. If he observes form as a practice of impermanence, he is not practicing Prajñāpāramitā. If he observes feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness as a practice of impermanence, he is not practicing Prajñāpāramitā.” The five appropriated aggregates, the five paths of rebirth, and various fivefold Dharma gateways such as these are also like this. The remaining sixfold, sevenfold, eightfold, and even immeasurable Dharma gateways are also like this.

Just as the Mahāprajñāpāramitā is immeasurable and boundless, the causes and conditions for teaching the Prajñāpāramitā are also immeasurable and boundless. Because this matter is vast, this is only a brief explanation of the arising causes and conditions of the Mahāprajñāpāramitā.

The Explanation of Thus: Faith as the Entrance to the Buddha Dharma

Chinese Source

摩訶般若波羅蜜初品 如是我聞一時釋論第二

〖經〗如是我聞：一時。

〖論〗問曰：諸佛經何以故初稱「如是」語？

Ru-Yi Translation

Chapter One of the Mahāprajñāpāramitā: Explanation of “Thus Have I Heard: At One Time,” Section Two

Sutra: Thus have I heard: at one time.

Treatise: Question: Why do the sutras of the Buddhas begin with the word “thus”?

答曰：佛法大海，信為能入，智為能度。「如是」義者，即是信。

若人心中有信清淨，是人能入佛法；若無信，是人不能入佛法。不信者言：「是事不如是」，是不信相。信者言：「是事如是。」

譬如牛皮未柔，不可屈折；無信人亦如是。

譬如牛皮已柔，隨用可作；有信人亦如是。

Answer: In the great ocean of the Buddha Dharma, faith is what enables one to enter, and wisdom is what enables one to cross over. The meaning of “thus” is faith.

If a person's mind has pure faith, that person can enter the Buddha Dharma. If there is no faith, that person cannot enter the Buddha Dharma. One who lacks faith says, “This matter is not thus.” This is the mark of disbelief. One who has faith says, “This matter is thus.”

It is like oxhide that has not yet been softened: it cannot be bent or folded. A person without faith is the same.

It is like oxhide that has already been softened: it can be made into whatever is needed. A person with faith is the same.

復次，經中說：「信如手，如人有手，入寶山中，自在取寶；有信亦如是，入佛法無漏根、力、覺、道、禪定寶山中，自在所取。無信如無手，無手人入寶山中，則不能有所取。無信亦如是，入佛法寶山，都無所得。」

佛言：「若人有信，是人能入我大法海中，能得沙門果，不空剃頭染袈裟。若無信，是人不能入我法海中，如枯樹不生華實，不得沙門果，雖剃頭染衣，讀種種經，能難能答，於佛法中空無所得。」以是故，如是義在佛法初，善信相故。

Furthermore, it says in the sutras: “Faith is like a hand. Just as a person with hands can enter a mountain of jewels and freely take jewels, a person with faith can enter the jewel mountain of the Buddha Dharma, with its undefiled faculties, powers, factors of awakening, path, and meditative concentrations, and freely take what is there. Lack of faith is like having no hands. If a person without hands enters a mountain of jewels, that person cannot take anything. Lack of faith is the same: one enters the jewel mountain of the Buddha Dharma and obtains nothing at all.”

The Buddha said, “If a person has faith, that person can enter my great ocean of Dharma and attain the fruits of renunciation. Such a person does not shave the head and dye the robe in vain. If there is no faith, that person cannot enter my ocean of Dharma. Like a withered tree that produces no flowers or fruit, that person cannot attain the fruits of renunciation. Even if such a person shaves the head, dyes the robe, reads many sutras, and is able to challenge and answer others, within the Buddha Dharma that person gains nothing at all.” Therefore the meaning of “thus” stands at the beginning of the Buddha Dharma, because it is the mark of wholesome faith.

復次，佛法深遠，更有佛乃能知。

人有信者，雖未作佛，以信力故能入佛法。如梵天王請佛初轉法輪，以偈請佛：「閻浮提先出，多諸不淨法；願開甘露門，當說清淨道！」

佛以偈答：「我法甚難得，能斷諸結使，三有愛著心，是人不能解！」

Furthermore, the Buddha Dharma is deep and far-reaching; only another Buddha can fully know it.

If a person has faith, then even though that person has not yet become a Buddha, by the power of faith one can enter the Buddha Dharma. For example, when Great Brahmā King requested the Buddha to turn the wheel of Dharma for the first time, he requested him with a verse: “In Jambudvīpa there have long appeared many impure dharmas. Please open the gate of nectar and teach the pure path!”

The Buddha replied in verse: “My Dharma is extremely hard to obtain; it can cut off the fetters. Those whose minds are attached to love for the three realms cannot understand it.”

梵天王白佛：「大德！世界中智，有上、中、下。善濡直心者，易可得度，是人若不聞法者，退墮諸惡難中，譬如水中蓮華，有生有熟，有水中未出者，若不得日光則不能開。佛亦如是，佛以大慈悲憐愍眾生，故為說法。」

佛念過去、未來、現在三世諸佛法，皆度眾生為說法，我亦應爾。如是思惟竟，受梵天王等諸天請說法。爾時，世尊以偈答曰：「我今開甘露味門，若有信者得歡喜；於諸人中說妙法，非惱他故而為說。」

Great Brahmā King said to the Buddha, “Great Virtuous One, in the world there are beings of superior, middle, and inferior wisdom. Those whose minds are gentle, soft, and upright can easily be liberated. If such people do not hear the Dharma, they will fall back into evil difficulties. They are like lotuses in the water: some are young, some are mature, and some have not yet emerged from the water. If they do not receive sunlight, they cannot open. The Buddha is also like this. Because the Buddha has great loving-kindness and compassion and takes pity on beings, he teaches the Dharma.”

The Buddha reflected, “The Dharma of all Buddhas of the past, future, and present is to teach Dharma in order to liberate beings. I too should do this.” After reflecting in this way, he accepted the request of Great Brahmā King and the other devas to teach the Dharma. At that time the World-Honored One replied in verse: “I now open the gate to the taste of nectar. Those who have faith will gain joy. Among human beings I teach the wondrous Dharma; I do not teach it in order to trouble others.”

佛此偈中，不說布施人得歡喜，亦不說多聞、持戒、忍辱、精進、禪定、智慧人得歡喜，獨說信人。佛意如是：我第一甚深法微妙，無量無數，不可思議，不動不猗不著，無所得法，非一切智人則不能解。

是故佛法中信力為初，信力能入，非布施、持戒、禪定、智慧等能初入佛法。如說偈言：「世間人心動，愛好福果報，而不好福因，求有不求滅。先聞邪見法，心著而深入，我此甚深法，無信云何解？」

In this verse the Buddha does not say that people who give will gain joy. Nor does he say that those who are learned, keep precepts, practice patience, vigor, meditative concentration, or wisdom will gain joy. He speaks only of people who have faith. The Buddha's intention is this: “My foremost, extremely deep Dharma is subtle, immeasurable, countless, inconceivable, unmoving, unsupported, unattached, and without anything to be obtained. Those who are not omniscient cannot understand it.”

Therefore in the Buddha Dharma, the power of faith comes first. The power of faith enables one to enter. Giving, keeping precepts, meditative concentration, wisdom, and so on are not what first enable one to enter the Buddha Dharma. As a verse says: “People of the world have agitated minds. They delight in the fruits of merit but do not delight in the causes of merit. They seek existence and do not seek cessation. Having first heard dharmas of wrong view, their minds become attached and enter deeply into them. Without faith, how could they understand this deep Dharma of mine?”

如提婆達大弟子俱迦梨等，無信法故，墮惡道中。是人無信，於佛法自以智慧求不能得。何以故？佛法甚深故。

如梵天王教俱迦梨說偈：「欲量無量法，智者所不量，無量法欲量，此人自覆沒！」

For example, Devadatta's chief disciple Kokalika and others, because they had no faith in the Dharma, fell into the bad destinies. Such people lack faith. Even if they seek within the Buddha Dharma by their own wisdom, they cannot obtain it. Why? Because the Buddha Dharma is extremely deep.

As Great Brahmā King instructed Kokalika in verse: “If one wants to measure the immeasurable Dharma, the wise do not measure it. When a person wants to measure the immeasurable Dharma, that person buries himself.”

復次，「如是」義者，若人心善直信，是人可聽法，若無是相則不解。如所說偈：「聽者端視如渴飲，一心入於語議中，踊躍聞法心悲喜，如是之人應為說。」

復次，「如是」義在佛法初，現世利、後世利、涅槃利、諸利根本，信為大力。

Furthermore, the meaning of “thus” is this: if a person's mind is wholesome, upright, and faithful, that person can listen to the Dharma. If this mark is absent, the person will not understand. As a verse says: “The listener gazes steadily, as if drinking while thirsty; with one mind he enters into the words and their meaning. Leaping with joy on hearing the Dharma, his heart is moved with gladness and sorrow. Such a person should be taught.”

Furthermore, the meaning of “thus” stands at the beginning of the Buddha Dharma because faith is a great power, the root of all benefits: benefit in this life, benefit in future lives, and the benefit of nirvāṇa.

復次，一切諸外道出家，心念我法微妙，第一清淨。如是人自歎所行法，毀他人法，是故現世相打鬥諍，後世墮地獄，受種種無量苦。如說偈：「自法愛染故，呰毀他人法，雖持戒行人，不脫地獄苦！」

是佛法中，棄捨一切愛，一切見，一切吾我憍慢，悉斷不著。如《[木*伐]喻經》言：「汝曹若解我[木*伐]喻法，是時善法應棄捨，何況不善法？」

佛自於般若波羅蜜，不念不猗，何況餘法有猗著者？以是故，佛法初頭稱「如是」。

Furthermore, all non-Buddhist renunciants think in their minds, “My Dharma is subtle, wondrous, and foremost in purity.” Such people praise the Dharma they practice and disparage the Dharma of others. For this reason, in the present life they strike one another and enter into fights and disputes; in future lives they fall into hell and experience many kinds of immeasurable suffering. As a verse says: “Because one is stained by love for one's own Dharma, one scorns and slanders the Dharma of others. Even if one is a person who keeps precepts and practices, one does not escape the suffering of hell.”

Within this Buddha Dharma, all craving, all views, and all arrogance of “I” and “mine” are abandoned, completely cut off, and not clung to. As the Raft Simile Sutra says: “If you understand my Dharma of the raft simile, then even wholesome dharmas should be abandoned, to say nothing of unwholesome dharmas.”

The Buddha himself does not think of or rely upon Prajñāpāramitā. How much less should there be reliance or attachment to any other Dharma? For this reason, the beginning of the Buddha Dharma is called “thus.”

佛意如是：我弟子無愛法，無染法，無朋黨，但求離苦解脫，不戲論諸法相。如說《阿他婆耆經》摩犍提難偈言：「決定諸法中，橫生種種想，悉捨內外故，云何當得道？」

佛答言：「非見聞知覺，亦非持戒得；非不見聞等，非不持戒得。如是論悉捨，亦捨我我所，不取諸法相，如是可得道。」

摩犍提問曰：「若不見聞等，亦非持戒得；非不見聞等，非不持戒得。如我心觀察，持啞法得道！」

佛答言：「汝依邪見門，我知汝癡道。汝不見妄想，爾時自當啞！」

The Buddha's intention is this: “My disciples have no Dharma of craving, no Dharma of defilement, and no factional party. They only seek liberation from suffering and do not conceptually elaborate upon the marks of dharmas.” As the challenging verses of Māgandiya in the Atthakavagga Sutra say: “If, amid determinate dharmas, one gives rise sideways to many kinds of perceptions, and if all internal and external things are abandoned, how then can the path be attained?”

The Buddha answered: “It is not attained by seeing, hearing, knowing, or perceiving, nor is it attained by keeping precepts. It is not attained by not seeing and not hearing, and so on, nor is it attained by not keeping precepts. All such doctrines are abandoned, and ‘I’ and ‘mine’ are also abandoned. Without grasping the marks of dharmas, in this way the path can be attained.”

Māgandiya asked: “If it is not attained by seeing and hearing, and so on, nor by keeping precepts; and if it is not attained by not seeing and hearing, and so on, nor by not keeping precepts, then as I observe it in my mind, one attains the path by maintaining silence!”

The Buddha answered: “You rely on the gateway of wrong views. I know your deluded path. When you do not see false imagination, at that time you will naturally be silent.”

復次，我法真實，餘法妄語，我法第一，餘法不實，是為鬥諍本。今「如是」義，示人無諍法，聞他所說，說人無咎。以是故，諸佛經初稱「如是」。略說「如是」義竟。

Furthermore, saying, “My Dharma is true, while other Dharmas are false speech; my Dharma is foremost, while other Dharmas are not real,” is the root of fighting and dispute. Now the meaning of “thus” shows people the Dharma without dispute. When one hears what another says, one does not declare that person at fault. For this reason, the sutras of the Buddhas begin with “thus.” This is the brief explanation of the meaning of “thus.”

The Explanation of I and Heard: Conventional Speech and Dependent Hearing

Chinese Source

「我」者，今當說。

問曰：若佛法中言一切法空，一切無有吾我，云何佛經初頭言「如是我聞」？

Ru-Yi Translation

Now the word “I” will be explained.

Question: If in the Buddha Dharma it is said that all dharmas are empty and that there is no “I” or “mine” at all, why do the Buddha's sutras begin by saying, “Thus have I heard”?

答曰：佛弟子輩雖知無我，隨俗法說我，非實我也。譬如以金錢買銅錢，人無笑者。何以故？賣買法應爾。言「我」者亦如是，於無我法中而說我，隨世俗故不應難。如《天問經》中偈說：「有羅漢比丘，諸漏已永盡，於最後邊身，能言吾我不？」

佛答言：「有羅漢比丘，諸漏已永盡，於最後邊身，能言有吾我。」

世界法中說我，非第一實義中說。

以是故，諸法空無我，世界法故，雖說我，無咎。

Answer: Although the Buddha's disciples know no-self, they speak of “I” in accordance with conventional worldly usage. It is not a real self. It is like buying copper coins with gold coins: no one laughs at this. Why? Because that is how buying and selling are done. Speaking of “I” is the same. Within the Dharma of no-self, one speaks of “I” in accordance with worldly convention, so this should not be challenged. As a verse in the Sutra of Questions from the Devas says: “There is an arhat monk whose outflows have been ended forever. In his final body, can he speak of ‘I’ and ‘mine’?”

The Buddha answered, “There is an arhat monk whose outflows have been ended forever. In his final body, he can speak of ‘I’ and ‘mine.’”

“I” is spoken within worldly Dharma, not within the ultimate meaning.

Therefore, although all dharmas are empty and without self, because it is worldly Dharma, there is no fault in speaking of “I.”

復次，世界語言有三根本：一者、邪見，二者、慢，三者、名字。是中二種不淨，一種淨。

一切凡人三種語：邪、慢、名字；見道學人二種語：慢、名字；諸聖人一種語：名字。內心雖不違實法，而隨世界人故共傳是語，除世界邪見，故隨俗無諍。以是故，除二種不淨語，本隨世故用一種語。佛弟子隨俗故說我，無有咎。

Furthermore, worldly language has three roots: first, wrong views; second, conceit; and third, names. Among these, two are impure and one is pure.

All ordinary people have three kinds of speech: wrong views, conceit, and names. Learners who have seen the path have two kinds of speech: conceit and names. Noble ones have one kind of speech: names. Although inwardly their minds do not contradict the true Dharma, because they follow people of the world, they transmit this speech together with them. They remove worldly wrong views and therefore follow convention without dispute. For this reason, after removing the two kinds of impure speech, they use the one kind of speech that originally follows the world. The Buddha's disciples speak of “I” in accordance with convention, and there is no fault.

復次，若人著無吾我相言是實，餘妄語，是人應難：「汝一切法實相無我，云何言如是我聞？」今諸佛弟子，一切法空無所有，是中心不著，亦不言著諸法實相，何況無我法中心著？以是故，不應難言何以說我。如《中論》中偈說：「若有所不空，應當有所空，不空尚不得，何況得於空？

凡人見不空，亦復見於空，不見見無見，是實名涅槃。

非二安隱門，能破諸邪見；諸佛所行處，是名無我法。」

略說「我」義竟。

Furthermore, if someone clings to the mark of no “I” and no “mine” and says, “This is real; all the rest is false speech,” then that person may rightly challenge, “You say that the true character of all dharmas is no-self. How can you say, ‘Thus have I heard’?” But now the disciples of the Buddhas know that all dharmas are empty and without anything at all. Their minds do not cling within this; nor do they say that they cling to the true character of all dharmas. How much less would their minds cling within the Dharma of no-self? For this reason, one should not challenge them by asking why they speak of “I.” As verses in the Mūlamadhyamakakārikā say: “If there were something not empty, there should be something empty. Since even the not-empty cannot be obtained, how much less can emptiness be obtained?

Ordinary people see the not-empty and also see emptiness. Not seeing views and not seeing non-views: this is truly called nirvāṇa.

The peaceful gateway free from duality can break all wrong views. The place practiced by the Buddhas is called the Dharma of no-self.”

This is the brief explanation of the meaning of “I.”

「聞」者，今當說。

問曰：「聞」者，云何聞？用耳根聞耶？用耳識聞？用意識聞耶？若耳根聞，耳根無覺知故，不應聞。 若耳識聞，耳識一念故不能分別，不應聞。 若意識聞，意識亦不能聞。何以故？先五識識五塵，然後意識識；意識不能識現在五塵，唯識過去、未來五塵。若意識能識現在五塵者，盲聾人亦應識聲色。何以故？意識不破故。

Now the word “heard” will be explained.

Question: As for “heard,” how does hearing occur? Is it heard by the ear faculty? Is it heard by ear-consciousness? Is it heard by mind-consciousness? If the ear faculty hears, then because the ear faculty has no awareness or knowing, it should not be able to hear. If ear-consciousness hears, then because ear-consciousness lasts for only one moment and cannot discriminate, it should not be able to hear. If mind-consciousness hears, then mind-consciousness also cannot hear. Why? First the five consciousnesses cognize the five objects, and only afterward mind-consciousness cognizes them. Mind-consciousness cannot cognize the five present objects; it only cognizes the five objects of the past and future. If mind-consciousness could cognize the five present objects, then blind and deaf people should also cognize sounds and forms. Why? Because their mind-consciousness is not impaired.

答曰：非耳根能聞聲，亦非耳識，亦非意識。能聞聲事，從多因緣和合故得聞聲，不得言一法能聞聲。何以故？耳根無覺故，不應聞聲；識無色無對無處故，亦不應聞聲；聲無覺亦無根故，不能知聲。爾時，耳根不破，聲至可聞處，意欲聞。情、塵、意和合故耳識生，隨耳識即生意識，能分別種種因緣得聞聲。以是故，不應作是難：「誰聞聲？」

Answer: It is not the ear faculty that can hear sound, nor is it ear-consciousness, nor is it mind-consciousness. The event of being able to hear sound occurs because many causes and conditions come together. One cannot say that a single dharma is able to hear sound. Why? Since the ear faculty has no awareness, it should not hear sound. Since consciousness has no form, no resistance, and no location, it also should not hear sound. Since sound has no awareness and also has no faculty, it cannot know sound. At that time, when the ear faculty is unimpaired, sound reaches a place where it can be heard, and the mind wishes to hear. Because faculty, object, and attention come together, ear-consciousness arises; immediately following ear-consciousness, mind-consciousness arises and is able to discriminate the many causes and conditions through which sound is heard. For this reason, one should not pose the challenge, “Who hears the sound?”

佛法中亦無有一法能作、能見、能知。如說偈：「有業亦有果，無作業果者，此第一甚深，是法佛能見。

雖空亦不斷，相續亦不常，罪福亦不失，如是法佛說。」

略說「聞」竟。

In the Buddha Dharma, there is also no single dharma that can act, see, or know. As verses say: “There is karma and there are also results, yet there is no maker of karma and results. This is foremost and extremely deep; this Dharma is what the Buddha can see.

Although empty, it is not annihilated; although continuous, it is not permanent. Misdeeds and merit are also not lost. Such is the Dharma taught by the Buddha.”

This is the brief explanation of “heard.”
