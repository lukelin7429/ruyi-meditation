The Discourses

The discourses of the Buddha (suttas), preserved for two and a half millennia in the Pali Canon — and explained for the English-speaking practitioner, without hiding behind jargon.

We are working through the canon one collection at a time. Right now: the Majjhima Nikāya — the "middle-length discourses" — 152 dialogues that include some of the most luminous and practical of the Buddha's teachings.

Each entry below pairs the discourse itself with a plain-English explanation written for modern readers. We do not assume you know the technical vocabulary; where we have to use a Pali term, we say what it means.

A note on this section. Our current Majjhima Nikāya commentary lives on our sister project academy.ruyimeditation.org. We are in the process of bringing it home to this site. The links below take you there for now.
