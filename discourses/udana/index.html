Udāna

Heartfelt Sayings

Part of the Khuddaka Nikāya, the Udāna collects eighty short episodes from the Buddha's life, each closing with a spontaneous "heartfelt saying" — a spoken or verse utterance the Buddha makes not because he was asked, but because a moment moved him to speak. Most are brief: a short narrative frame followed by a few lines of prose or verse. Together they range from his first week after awakening to the schism at Kosambī to a series of profound reflections on Nibbāna itself.

This collection includes some of the most quietly powerful texts in the canon — the Buddha's account of his own first days of freedom, the story of Bāhiya's instant awakening ("in the seen, let there only be the seen"), and four short, direct statements on what extinguishment actually is. Each entry here has the same reading guide, full Sujato translation, and self-check quiz as our other series.
