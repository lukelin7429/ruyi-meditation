Five days. Five small lessons.

The children's camp keeps the same heart as the youth camp — kindness, gratitude, honest character — but moves at a child's pace. Mornings are gentle: a short chant, some stretching, breakfast together. The days fill up with stories about the Buddha and his disciples, painting and stone-craft, singing, a dessert to make with their own hands, and plenty of time outdoors.

Each day carries one simple idea. Together the five days form a small arc: from meeting the Three Jewels, to thanking the natural world, to sharing with kindness, to building good habits, and finally to bringing it all home to family.
