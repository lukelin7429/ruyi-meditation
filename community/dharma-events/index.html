Every month

The monthly retreat.

Once a month, practitioners climb the mountain to Ru-Yi for a full day of practice together — the steady heartbeat of our community. We come down from the busyness of the world and spend a day doing nothing but turning toward the Dharma.

The day unfolds gently: we recite the name of the Buddha together, sit in periods of quiet meditation, listen to a short teaching, and share a simple vegetarian meal. No experience is needed — only a willingness to be present. In winter, plum blossoms open white across the slopes and mountain mist drifts through the courtyard.

Free · open to everyone

The year's festivals

Seasons of gathering.

Around the monthly rhythm, a handful of larger observances mark the turning of the year.

Spring · Vesak

Bathing the Buddha

浴佛節 · The Buddha's Birthday

On the Buddha's birthday we pour fragrant water over the infant Buddha — an old and tender rite of purifying the heart, celebrated together in the main hall.

Summer · July

Youth & Children's Camps

學佛營 · A few days away from screens

Each summer the mountain fills with young people for a few free days of meditation, nature, stories, and character — the next generation learning to steady the mind.

Autumn · Ullambana

The Ullambana Assembly

盂蘭盆法會 · Gratitude to our ancestors

In the seventh lunar month we gather to remember and give thanks to our parents and ancestors — the festival of filial love and the transfer of merit.

Winter · New Year

New Year Homecoming

回娘家 · Returning home to the mountain

As the lunar year turns, old friends of the monastery come back up the mountain to begin the year in practice, blessing, and good company.
