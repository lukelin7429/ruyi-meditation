Mindfulness and the Present Moment

正念與當下 · Sati 念 · 平靜心猿意馬
Principle  Mindfulness · Sati 正念 English  Present Continuous · “I am + V-ing” Level  Ages 5–16

This Week’s Path

Overview 本週重點

This week we learn to come home to the present moment. Our restless, jumping mind — the “Monkey Brain” 心猿意馬 — calms down when we simply notice what is happening right now. In English, the perfect tool for “right now” is the Present Continuous tense.

English Goal

英文目標

Use the Present Continuous tense (“I am + V-ing”) to describe what is happening right now, and learn key vocabulary for calm and mindfulness: stress, calm, deep breathing, peace, mindful, technique.

Spiritual Goal

佛學／心靈目標

Understand Mindfulness (Sati) as paying attention to the present moment without judgment. Practice the Mind-Body-Heart connection and deep breathing to settle the restless “Monkey Brain” and ease stress (Dukkha).

Sentence Patterns

句型 · Key Patterns
Pattern 1 · What I am doing right now
I am ___ing.
·I am breathing slowly.
·I am sitting quietly.
·I am feeling calm now.
Pattern 2 · He / She / It is doing
The ___ is ___ing.
·The monkey is jumping.
·My mind is racing today.
Pattern 3 · Asking and answering about now
What are you doing? — I am ___ing.
·What are you doing when you feel calm?
·I am drawing, and I am taking deep breaths.

Grammar Focus · Present Continuous (現在進行式)

We use the Present Continuous to talk about what is happening right now — the present moment. The structure is Subject + am / is / are + V-ing: I am breathing. · She is sitting. · The monkey is jumping. The V-ing form means “正在做…”. Use am with I, is with he / she / it, and are with you / we / they.

The Story: Calming the Monkey Brain

平靜心猿意馬

Imagine a little monkey living inside your head （心猿意馬）. It never sits still. It jumps from branch to branch — one moment thinking about lunch, the next about a test, the next about a game. This restless, jumping mind is what we call the “Monkey Brain.”

When the monkey jumps too much, we feel stress. Our chest gets tight and our thoughts race. But we do not need to fight the monkey or chase it. We only need to do one quiet thing: notice it. “Oh — my mind is jumping right now.” That noticing, without judging, is Mindfulness (Sati).

Then we take a slow deep breath. We place one hand on our mind, one on our body, one on our heart, and we feel them come together in the present moment. Little by little, the monkey grows quiet, sits down, and rests. We are calm — not because nothing is happening, but because we are fully here, right now.

The Principle: Mindfulness · Sati 正念

Mindfulness (Sati) is paying attention to the present moment without judgment. We are not trying to make the mind empty or to punish ourselves for being distracted. We simply notice what the mind and body are doing right now — and gently return, again and again, to the breath. This is also Right Effort: deep breathing is a wholesome action that quietly replaces restless stress with peace. When we live in the present moment, much of our suffering (Dukkha) softens on its own.

Practice: Calmness Journal

平靜反思日記 · Homework

Each day this week, journal about one moment of stress and one technique you used to feel calm — using the Present Continuous tense. Choose the tier that fits you. 每天寫一篇，用現在進行式記錄一次壓力與一個平靜的方法。

Beginner 入門

2 sentences

Ages 5–8 · new learners
Write 2 sentences. Use a V-ing form once, and use the word calm or peace. Then draw a picture of your “calm feeling” (or a settled monkey).
ExampleI feel calm now. I am breathing deeply.
Intermediate 中級

4–5 sentences

Ages 9–12 · developing fluency
Write 4–5 sentences using at least 3 new words (stress, deep breathing, mindful). Describe one stressor and one calm action with V-ing.
ExampleToday, I was feeling stress because I was doing my math homework. Now, I am sitting quietly. I am mindful of my body.
Advanced 進階

5+ sentences

Ages 13–16 · fluent speakers
Write 5+ sentences with a complex structure (when, while, although) and a personal 3-step calmness strategy. Explain your “Monkey Brain.”
ExampleMy Monkey Brain is jumping a lot when I am preparing for tests. First, I am taking deep breaths. Secondly, I am stopping my thoughts. Lastly, I am smiling to release the pressure.
