Quarter 2 · Week 22 · Character & Connection

Moving in Harmony

在和諧中律動 · The Body’s Grace 身體的優雅
Principle  Moving Meditation · Body as Medicine 身體即良藥 English  “I feel harmony…” · “Everyone is unique.” Level  Ages 5–16

This Week’s Path

Overview 本週重點

This week the body becomes the teacher. Through gentle, advanced Tai Chi we turn quiet meditation into flowing movement, and we learn that harmony is the body, breath, and group moving as one. We also celebrate a beautiful truth: every single person is unique.

English Goal

英文目標

Learn the words harmony and unique, and correct the spelling of wonderful and excellent. Describe inner states with “I feel harmony in my heart” and “Everyone is unique.”

Spiritual Goal

佛學／心靈目標

Understand harmony as body and mind in agreement, and deepen the idea of the body as medicine. Through Anattā (無我), honor that each being is unique, yet all can live together in peace.

Sentence Patterns

句型 · Key Patterns
Pattern 1 · Feeling harmony inside
I feel harmony in ___.
·I feel harmony in my heart.
·I feel calm when I move slowly.
·I move in harmony with the class.
Pattern 2 · Everyone is special
___ is unique.
·Everyone is unique.
·My painting is unique because I used my favorite colors.
Pattern 3 · Living together (noun usage)
We live in harmony.
·Our class sings in harmony together.
·Unique people can live in harmony.

Grammar Focus · Adjective vs. noun

Unique is an adjective — it describes a person or thing: Everyone is unique. Harmony is a noun — a thing we have or live in: We live in harmony. Watch your spelling, too: it is w-o-n-d-e-r-f-u-l and e-x-c-e-l-l-e-n-t.

The Principle: Moving in Harmony

身體良藥 · Moving Meditation

One day the teacher asked the students to sit as silent as a mountain. First they listened to their own breathing. Then the teacher began to speak softly — a guided meditation — and the children let the calm voice lead their minds back to the present moment.

Next, the teacher stood up and led a slow Tai Chi flow. Arms lifted like rising water; feet stepped like floating clouds. Every child moved differently, yet the whole class flowed together — this is harmony. The teacher smiled and said, “When your body and your breath agree, you have found harmony in motion.”

The teacher reminded them: each child is unique — no one else in the world moves, breathes, or thinks in exactly the same way. And still, all these unique bodies can move as one. That is the gift of the body as medicine: a healthy body and a calm breath become the home of a peaceful heart.

Check your understanding

  • What does “harmony in motion” mean? 「律動中的和諧」是什麼意思？
  • How did the guided meditation help the students? 老師的引導冥想如何幫助學生？
  • Why is it good that everyone is unique? 每個人都獨一無二，為什麼是好事？

Body, Breath, and Group as One 身心一致

Harmony is not everyone being the same. It is many unique people — each with their own佛性 (Buddha-nature) — choosing to move, breathe, and live together gently. When your body is well and your breath is calm, the mind rests easily. Move your body with kindness, and the harmony you feel inside will spread to your family and your school.

Practice: Unique Harmony Sentences

作業 · Homework

Write your sentences this week, and practice 3 minutes of silent meditation at home — sit as still as a mountain. Choose the tier that fits you. 選一個適合自己的等級。

Beginner 入門

Draw & trace

Ages 5–8 · new learners
Draw a picture of yourself and write “I am unique.” Then trace and complete two sentences about harmony.
ExampleI want harmony in my world. Everyone is unique.
Intermediate 中級

Spelling & reflection

Ages 9–12 · developing fluency
Write wonderful, excellent, unique, harmony three times each. Then write 3 sentences about the guided meditation using the word silent.
ExampleI sat silent and calm. The teacher’s voice was wonderful. I felt harmony in my heart.
Advanced 進階

Integration paragraph

Ages 13–16 · fluent speakers
Write a 6–10 sentence paragraph using all four words. Explain why unique people should live in harmony, using the Tai Chi class as your example.
ExampleAlthough we are each unique, our class moved as one in the Tai Chi flow. That excellent, wonderful feeling was harmony in motion.
