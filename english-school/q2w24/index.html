The Prince's Great Journey

悉達多太子的人生旅程 · From Palace to Peace 從皇宮到平安
Principle  Renunciation & Purpose · 離欲與使命 English  “I find … interesting because …” Level  Ages 5–16

This Week’s Path

Overview 本週重點

This is the final lesson of our year together. We follow a young prince named Siddhartha, who had everything money could buy — yet went on a great journey to find something far more precious: a true purpose and a happiness that never fades.

English Goal

英文目標

Learn biography and purpose words: wealthy, journey, purpose, Dharma Wheel. Use the pattern “I find ___ interesting because ___” to share an opinion, and describe a story in the simple past tense.

Spiritual Goal

佛學／心靈目標

Understand why Prince Siddhartha left a wealthy palace to seek a purpose, and see the Dharma Wheel as the map that guides the whole journey. Comfort and riches are not the same as lasting happiness.

Sentence Patterns

句型 · Key Patterns
Pattern 1 · Sharing an opinion
I find ___ interesting because ___.
·I find the prince’s journey interesting because he gave up everything.
·I find the Dharma Wheel interesting because it is like a map.
Pattern 2 · Describing the past
He was ___. He wanted ___.
·The prince was very wealthy.
·He wanted to help all people end their suffering.
Pattern 3 · Talking about purpose
My purpose is to ___.
·My purpose is to learn and to be kind.
·The Dharma Wheel is his guide on the journey.

Grammar Focus · Simple past for telling a story

When we tell a story that already happened, we use the simple past tense. Many verbs add -ed: He wanted to leave. · She walked far. Some verbs change shape: be → was (He was wealthy), leave → left, see → saw (He saw an old man).

The Story: From Palace to Peace

悉達多太子的故事

Long ago, a baby prince named Siddhartha （悉達多） was born into a royal family. His father, the king, wanted his son to be happy forever, so he built a beautiful palace filled with gardens, music, and every treasure. The prince grew up wealthy, and he never saw anything sad. But inside his heart, he felt that something important was missing.

One day the prince rode outside the palace walls. For the first time he saw four sights: an old man, a sick man, a person who had died, and a calm wandering monk. The first three showed him that everyone grows old, gets sick, and dies — this is the suffering （苦） the palace had hidden from him. But the peaceful monk gave him hope: there might be a way to find true happiness.

So the prince made a brave choice. He left his palace, his riches, and his comfortable life to begin a great journey. His purpose was not to become rich, but to find a way to help all people be free from suffering. After years of searching, he sat quietly under a tree and looked deeply into his own mind — and there he found enlightenment （覺悟）. From that day he was called the Buddha, “the Awakened One,” and he taught the world the path of the Dharma Wheel （法輪）, the map that guides us home.

Check your understanding

  • Why did the king build such a beautiful palace? 國王為什麼蓋這麼美的皇宮？
  • What four sights did the prince see outside the walls? 太子在牆外看到哪四種景象？
  • What was the prince’s real purpose for leaving home? 太子離開家的真正目的是什麼？

The Principle: A True Purpose

使命與法輪 · Purpose & the Dharma Wheel

The prince teaches us that where we are going matters more than how fast we run. A right direction — the Dharma Wheel — is worth more than all the gold in a palace.

Direction over riches 方向勝過財富

Siddhartha had everything the world calls success, yet he was not at peace, because riches without a purpose are like an empty shell. When he chose to live for others — to help everyone end their suffering — his life finally had meaning. The Dharma Wheel is the map that keeps that journey on course. Even at school, your purpose can be simple and noble: to learn, to be kind, and to keep your inner peace.

Practice: My Story Reflection

影片心得 · Homework

Think about Prince Siddhartha’s journey. Choose the tier that fits you, and share what you found most interesting. 想一想太子的旅程，選一個適合自己的等級分享。

Beginner 入門

Draw & trace

Ages 5–8 · new learners
Draw the wealthy prince in his palace. Then trace and complete two sentences, and copy journey and purpose three times each.
ExampleThe Prince was very wealthy. The Dharma Wheel is his guide.
Intermediate 中級

3 sentences

Ages 9–12 · developing fluency
Write 3 sentences about the story. Use the pattern “I find ___ interesting because ___” and two vocabulary words. Then meditate for five minutes on your purpose at school.
ExampleI find the four sights interesting because they changed the prince. He was wealthy but he wanted to help others.
Advanced 進階

A short essay

Ages 13–16 · fluent speakers
Write a paragraph of 6–10 sentences. Explain why finding a purpose mattered more to Siddhartha than being wealthy, and how the Dharma Wheel acts as a map for his journey. Use all four vocabulary words.
ExampleAlthough the prince was wealthy, he felt empty until he found his purpose. The Dharma Wheel became the map for his long journey toward helping others.
